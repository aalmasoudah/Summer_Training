*   **Prioritize Protein:** Protein helps preserve muscle mass and keeps you feeling full. Include it at every meal.
*   **Eat Whole Foods:** Focus on vegetables, fruits, lean meats, whole grains, and healthy fats. Avoid ultra-processed foods which are often high in sugar and empty calories.

### 3. Tips for Success
1.  **Portion Control:** Use smaller plates and try to stop eating when you are 80% full.
2.  **Cook at Home:** Restaurant meals often contain more calories and sodium than home-cooked versions.
3.  **Sleep Well:** Lack of sleep increases hunger hormones (ghrelin) and decreases satiety hormones (leptin). Aim for 7–9 hours.
4.  **Move Your Body:** Combine diet with physical activity. Strength training helps build muscle, which boosts metabolism; cardio burns extra calories.
5.  **Track Progress:** Use a food diary or app to track what you eat, but focus more on habits than just the scale number.

### 4. Foods to Limit or Avoid
*   Sugary beverages (soda, sweetened juices, energy drinks).
*   Refined carbohydrates (white bread, white pasta, pastries).
*   Fried foods and processed snacks (chips, crackers).
*   Alcohol (it adds "empty" calories and lowers inhibitions regarding food choices).

### 5. Red Flags to Watch For
*   **Extreme Restriction:** Do not eat fewer than 1,200 calories per day without medical supervision. This can slow your metabolism and damage health.
*   **"Magic Bullet" Supplements:** Pills, teas, or powders that promise rapid weight loss are often ineffective or unsafe.
*   **All-or-Nothing Thinking:** If you slip up on a meal, don't give up for the rest of the day. Just get back on track with the next meal.

**Final Advice:** The best diet is the one you can stick to. Choose foods you enjoy, prepare them yourself, and be patient. Healthy weight loss typically happens at a rate of 0.5 to 2 pounds (0.2–1 kg) per week.
